About Me


Currently, I'm working as a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Center for Geospatial Sciences at the University of California, campus Riverside (UCR). My main interests comprises data analysis, data visualization, statistical modelling, open-source technologies (R, Python, RStudio, Jupyter Notebooks, etc.) and Big Data.

I earned a Ph.D. in Economics from the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio Grande do Sul (2017) (with Honorable Mention), a Master's in Statistics from the Federal University of Minas Gerais (2014) and a Bachelor in Statistics from the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (2010). Besides the academic career, I also worked as a researcher in statistics in the Statistics and Economics Foundation in southern Brazil for eight years and I was a professor of Data Science using the R programming language at the UniRitter university.

I've been working under the supervision of Serge Rey and my current research involves developing new methods of inference for nonspatial and spatial segregation measures, new methods for identifying and dealing with neighborhoods and data visualization for spatial data.
