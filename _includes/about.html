About Me.


Renan Xavier Cortes is a statistician, fascinated with statistics and data science.

Currently, he's working as a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Center for Geospatial Sciences at the University of California, campus Riverside (UCR). His main interests comprises data analysis, data visualization, statistical modelling, open-source technologies (R, Python, RStudio, Jupyter Notebooks, etc.) and Big Data.

He earned a Ph.D. in Economics from the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio Grande do Sul (2017), a Master's in Statistics from the Federal University of Minas Gerais (2014) and a Bachelor in Statistics from the Federal University of Rio Grande do Sul (2010). Besides the academic career, he also worked as a researcher in statistics in the Statistics and Economics Foundation in southern Brazil for eight years and was a professor of Data Science using the R programming language at the UniRitter university.

He's working under the supervision of Serge Rey and his current research involves developing new methods of inference for nonspatial and spatial segregation measures, new methods for identifying and dealing with neighborhoods and data visualization for spatial data.
